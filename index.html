Ortholinear keyboard trainer

I recently bought an ErgoDox EZ keyboard—my first ortholinear (rather than staggered) keyboard. The Oryx “Live Training” feature helps you learn to type on such a keyboard, but I needed something a little more focused to the task of getting used to straight columns.

So this is a whole-cloth imitation of the Live Training program, but instead of reciting “Alice's Adventures in Wonderland” or other static texts, this gives you a random series of columns.

It's a little bit specific to my layout, in two ways:

  • It's Dvorak. I haven't implemented support for QWERTY or other non-Dvorak layouts. (Pull requests welcome!)
  • My rightmost column is \-/. Trying to fit Dvorak into an ErgoDox requires some concessions and this was one of mine. It's probably one of the least contorted variants, but at any rate, your own translation of Dvorak to the ErgoDox (or your keyboard if it's something else) may be different. (Again, pull requests welcome!)
